Gorkha District, Nepal

Population: 288,134

Area: 3,610 square kilometers

Gorkha, a district in central Nepal, is renowned for its rich history, notably as the birthplace of Prithvi Narayan Shah, the unifier of Nepal. At the center of Gorkha stands the historic Gorkha Durbar, a hilltop palace offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes, serving as a testament to Nepal's royal legacy.

The cultural tapestry of Gorkha is diverse and vibrant, with traditional festivals like Dashain and Tihar celebrated with colorful rituals and fervor. Local cuisine reflects this diversity, with dishes infused with unique flavors and spices, offering visitors a taste of the region's culinary heritage.

Gorkha is also a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, serving as a gateway to the legendary Manaslu Circuit Trek. Trekkers traverse rugged terrain, passing through picturesque villages and pristine forests, all while enjoying breathtaking views of Mount Manaslu, the eighth highest peak in the world.
